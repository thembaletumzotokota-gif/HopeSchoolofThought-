About the Founder

Bishop T. Tokota (DipTh)

Hope School of Thought was established by Bishop T. Tokota (DipTh), a seasoned minister and theologian whose life's work has been dedicated to raising a generation of thinking, praying, and doctrinally grounded ministers of the Gospel.

Bishop Tokota is the author of Umshumayeli Nomyalezo — a landmark work in African ministerial literature meaning "The Preacher and His Message." In this foundational text, Bishop Tokota argues that the preacher and the message cannot be separated: the messenger must be transformed by the very Word he proclaims. This conviction is the philosophical and theological DNA of HST.

This college was born from the recognition that the African church needs ministers who do not merely repeat religious phrases but who understand the deep structures of Christian doctrine — ministers who can teach the faith, defend it intelligently, and apply it to the complex realities of contemporary life. The curriculum in Systematic and Dogmatic Theology, with a deep focus on Christology, represents Bishop Tokota's answer to that need.

HST stands for the conviction that right thinking about God produces right living for God. We invite you to think deeply, believe firmly, and serve faithfully.

"The preacher must not only know the message — he must be transformed by it. The messenger is the first congregation of every sermon he preaches."
— Bishop T. Tokota · Umshumayeli Nomyalezo
